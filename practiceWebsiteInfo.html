Girls Who Code

A program designed to close the gender gap in computer science by getting young girls interested in coding through clubs and immersion programs.

"Girls Who Code is on a mission to close the gender gap in technology and to change the image of what a programmer looks like and does."

About Me


My name is Phoebe Nicholas and I am in the Computer Academy at Oakland Technical High School. I plan to pursue computer science as a career and either focus on either cybersecurity or software engineering. In my free time, I volunteer at Chabot Space and Science Center, teach coding classes for a non-profit called Computing Minds, and in the summer of 2020 I also did an internship with Intel and Hack the Hood. The hardest part about being a girl interested in STEM thus far has been AP Physics where I am one of two girls in my class.
